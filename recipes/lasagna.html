Lasagna

Description

Lasagna is a classic Italian baked pasta dish made with wide, flat sheets of pasta layered with various fillings such as meat ragù, béchamel sauce, and cheeses like Parmesan, ricotta, and mozzarella, then baked until hot and bubbly.

The term "lasagna" can refer to both the individual pasta sheet and the entire dish, though in Italian, "lasagne" (plural) refers to the pasta sheets, while "lasagna" (singular) is used for the dish in southern Italy.

The dish is believed to have originated in Emilia-Romagna, particularly Bologna, where it is traditionally made with green pasta (colored with spinach) and layered with ragù and béchamel.

Ingredients

  • Barilla® Wavy Lasagne: ½ Box (approximately 12 sheets)
  • Barilla® Marinara Sauce: 2 Jars (approximately 32 oz or 946 ml total)
  • Bulk Italian Sausage or Ground Beef: 1 Pound (454 g)
  • Ricotta Cheese: 1 15-Oz Container (approximately 425 g)
  • Shredded Mozzarella Cheese: 4 Cups (16 oz or 454 g)
  • Parmigiano-Reggiano Cheese: ½ Cup (2 oz or 57 g), Grated
  • Eggs: 2, Lightly Beaten

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. Cook the lasagna noodles according to package instructions; drain and separate.
  3. In a large saucepan, combine the cooked meat and 1 ½ jars of sauce, simmer for 5 minutes.
  4. In a large bowl, combine the ricotta cheese, 3 ½ cups of mozzarella cheese, the grated Parmigiano-Reggiano cheese, and the lightly beaten eggs.
  5. Grease a 13 x 9 inch baking dish with cooking spray.
  6. Spread ¾ cup of the Barilla sauce over the bottom of the baking dish.
  7. Place 3-4 lasagne sheets on the bottom, slightly overlapping.
  8. Spread half of the cheese mixture over the lasagne and cover with ½ of the meat sauce.
  9. Repeat the layering process for two more layers.
  10. Cover the last layer with the remaining lasagne sheets and the remaining Barilla sauce.
  11. Sprinkle the remaining mozzarella cheese on top.
  12. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 30 minutes. Remove the foil and bake for an additional 10 minutes.
  13. Let the lasagna stand for 10 minutes before serving to allow it to set.
